Food

  • Banh Trang Tron Rice Paper Salad

    Bánh tráng refers to the pieces of rice paper (steamed rice batter that's been sun dried). It is cut up into strips and tossed with a tasty mixture, or trộn in Vietnamese, of ingredients that usually include: dried shrimp, peanuts, Vietnamese coriander, hard boiled quail eggs, shredded green mango, Vietnamese style beef jerky, dried garlic, lime juice, and chili paste.

    The results are a spicy, tangy mixture with a vast array of textures. This dish is almost exclusively sold on the streets. It's popular with students as it's quick and inexpensive (usually 10,000 dong).

  • WHAT IS NEM NƯỚNG?

    Nem nướng is a Vietnamese dish made from meatballs or pork sausages skewered and grilled. This preparation is strongly flavored with garlic and fish sauce. The presence of sugar brings a sweet and slightly caramelized aroma that is very pleasant.

    People accompany these skewers with rice paper wrappers that each guest can garnish at will with raw vegetables and vermicelli, and dip in a peanut sauce. The whole thing is quite refreshing and filling, and the assembly stage makes it a very convivial dish to share.

  • Banh Cuon – Vietnamese Incredibly Popular Street Food

    Banh Cuon is an endless list of Vietnamese rolls and wrap dishes, which requires a mixed variety of ingredients rolled in a sheet or vegetable. The uniqueness of these dishes often depends on the locality where the dish is prepared.

    The Món cuốn represents a rich diversity of the Vietnamese and their skillfulness with such delicate dishes.
